Quick answer

The Lechuza Cubico Color self-watering planter is our top pick for its durable fiberclay build, reliable water reservoir, and clean modern look that works with almost any outdoor space.

The Pottery Barn Cortina stone composite planter is an excellent runner-up if you prefer a naturally textured finish that patinas over time without the weight of real stone.

Outdoor planters do more than hold dirt, they define the shape of your patio, balcony, or garden. The right planter keeps plants healthy through proper drainage and insulation, while the wrong one fades, cracks, or traps roots. Material matters most: fiberclay and stone composites handle freeze-thaw cycles well, plastic is lightweight and budget-friendly but can degrade in sun, and ceramic offers beauty but needs protection in cold climates. Size and drainage holes are non-negotiable, most plants need at least one good drainage hole per square foot of surface area. Your choice also depends on how much maintenance you want. Self-watering planters with built-in reservoirs reduce watering frequency dramatically, while traditional pots demand more attention. Don’t overlook weight: a large filled planter can be very heavy, so consider whether you’ll ever need to move it.
